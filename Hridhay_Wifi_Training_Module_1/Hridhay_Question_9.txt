Backhaul is defined as the set of copper, fiber, or wireless links that connect the core (or backbone) networks with the smaller subnetworks towards the edge before users can access the internet by accessing the subnetwork. 

The following are types of wifi backhaul connectivity:

Fiber-Optic Backhaul
DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) Backhaul
Cable Broadband Backhaul
Satellite Backhaul
Microwave Backhaul
Cellular (4G/5G) Backhaul
Wireless Mesh Backhaul

House Backhaul is a Cable backhaul
